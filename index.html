Kids' Interactive Song Maker - Reflection

Guiding Values and UI Design

My design focused on playfulness, accessibility, and immediate feedback. The interface encourages children to explore music visually and aurally. I used a colourful gradient background, large icon buttons for song selection, and animated shapes that respond to user interaction. The Bubblegum Sans font and playful icons reinforce a kid-friendly aesthetic. Volume control and mute functionality provide safe audio interaction.

Feedback, Information Design, Mapping, and Learnability

  • Feedback: Clicking or tapping creates sound and animated shapes, giving immediate auditory and visual confirmation. Volume changes update the icon in real time, and active songs are highlighted.
  • Information Design: Instructions appear briefly and fade out, keeping the interface uncluttered.
  • Mapping: Song buttons are at the top center, volume controls at the top right, and the interactive canvas fills the remaining area.
  • Learnability: Intuitive icons and minimal instructions make it easy for children to explore without prior guidance.

Significant Changes Since Work-in-Progress

I implemented the volume control with mute toggle, refined the shape animations for smoother motion, added fail-safe logic for icons, and ensured the audio context resumes reliably on interaction.

Production Timeline and Process

Development started with the p5.js canvas and basic interaction logic. The second phase focused on UI design and visual feedback. The final phase added advanced features like volume control, mute functionality, oscillator management, and responsive resizing. Features were tested iteratively to ensure accurate visual and audio feedback.

Most Proud Design Aspect

I am most proud of the integration of visual and auditory feedback. Each tap produces a dynamic, colourful shape with the correct note, making the interface engaging and fun.

Most Challenging Design Aspect

Managing audio context and oscillators was challenging. I had to ensure sounds stop correctly when muted, handle multiple oscillators, and resume the audio context across browsers.

Creative Prompt and Advanced Techniques

This project follows the No Text Characters prompt. Interaction relies on icons, shapes, and sound. I used Math.random() extensively to generate random colours, shapes, and positions, creating a unique experience with each interaction.
